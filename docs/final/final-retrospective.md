## Strengths
The following are what the team did well on this project:
- Equal committment to the project and each member's assigned role and weekly deliverables. This commitment was especially helpful when frictions came up throughout the project, as it created a willingness for each team member to address concerns and quickly resolve conflicts in a team meeting. Moreover, each member worked proactively at identifying root causes and future improvements to prevent the same frictions/conflicts from arising in the future.
- Incorporating testing throughout our feature implementations instead of at the end of a feature's completion.
- Delivery discipline by ensuring all weekly deliverables are completed by their deadlines.
- Everyone on the team stepping up to help and provide support when another team member was stuck or lacked resources for the development that they owned.
  
## Areas of Improvement
The following are what the team would change earlier next time:
- Inclusion of a wireframe and detailed explanation of scope for each feature before development and in the project proposal. 
- Inclusion of expectations and processes on how frontend and backend developers are to work together in regards to implementations and changes to code fixes in the team charter at the start of the project.

## Valuable Engineering Practice
The most valuable engineering practice adopted this semester was implementing unit and integration testings continuously throughout backend development, especially after each code change was made.

## Costly Mistake or Rework Point
Our most costly mistake or rework point delayed frontend from developing the registration and log-in features by approximately two weeks. The main causes were a misunderstanding of the simple flight search feature's scope and lack of communication regarding backend's changes to frontend's development of the feature.

## Project's Improvements
The project improved from proposal to final release by:
- Inclusion of a unique member-exclusive feature that is our destination suggestion feature.
- Inclusion of 6 search preferences for the destination suggestion form instead of the 3 that we had at the start.
- Inclusion of a drop-down destination field in the destination suggestion workflow that conveniently lists all of a user's suggested destinations instead of requiring them to type in the destination as we originally planned.
- PR formatting and process were more consistently adopted as we progressed.
- The team got better at navigating friction by being quicker to come together in a team meeting, discuss root cause(s) of our problems, and figure out scalable solutions forward.
